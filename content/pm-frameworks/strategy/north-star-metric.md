# North Star Metric

## Quick Overview

A North Star Metric is the single metric that best captures the core value your product delivers to customers. It aligns the entire organization around what matters most.

## Characteristics of a Good North Star

- **Reflects customer value**: Not just business outcome
- **Leading indicator**: Predicts future success
- **Measurable**: Can be tracked consistently
- **Actionable**: Teams can influence it
- **Understandable**: Everyone can grasp it

## Examples by Product Type

- **Spotify**: Time spent listening
- **Airbnb**: Nights booked
- **Facebook**: Daily active users
- **Slack**: Messages sent in team channels
- **Zoom**: Weekly meeting minutes hosted

## Common Anti-Patterns

❌ Revenue (lagging, not customer-focused)
❌ Signups (vanity metric)
❌ Page views (doesn't indicate value)

## When to Use

- Aligning teams around product strategy
- Prioritizing features based on impact
- Measuring product health
- Communicating success across organization

## Key Questions

- What moment represents value delivery to users?
- What behavior shows users got what they came for?
- What metric would we optimize if we could only choose one?
